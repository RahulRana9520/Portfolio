Dead Poets Society — Review

1. Your favourite dialogue and why?

"Carpe diem. Seize the day, boys. Make your lives extraordinary." — This line is unforgettable because it challenges us to break free from routines and truly live life with passion and purpose.

2. Your personal review

Dead Poets Society is an emotional and inspiring film that speaks to anyone who has struggled to find their voice. Robin Williams delivers a stellar performance that encourages students to think independently. It’s one of those rare films that makes you introspect and think deeply about your life choices.

3. Learning to take away from the movie

The importance of standing up for yourself and pursuing what genuinely matters to you. It taught me not to blindly follow societal expectations and to live with authenticity and courage.

4. Related concepts you've learned in the classroom

In our career development classes, we discuss leadership, creativity, and self-expression — themes echoed in John Keating’s teaching style. The movie reinforced how crucial it is to think critically and creatively, even in structured systems.
