Lecture 2: Responding to Interview Questions

In my second lecture, I learned how to effectively respond to challenging and tricky questions posed by interviewers. Key takeaways include:

  • Analyzing questions carefully before responding.
  • Structuring responses logically to maintain clarity.
  • Maintaining confidence while answering.
  • Understanding the importance of critical thinking.
  • Staying calm under pressure.
  • Highlighting strengths and problem-solving abilities in answers.
  • Gaining insights into common interview strategies.
  • Handling unexpected or difficult questions with composure.

This knowledge will greatly help me in future interviews by improving my ability to articulate well-thought-out responses.

Lecture 3: Effective Presentation Skills

In my third lecture, I learned the essential skills required to deliver an effective and well-structured presentation. Key takeaways include:

  • Managing time efficiently during presentations.
  • Handling questions from the audience with poise.
  • Using storytelling techniques to make presentations more compelling.

This knowledge will enable me to deliver presentations with clarity, confidence, and effectiveness in both academic and professional settings.

Assignment 1

Q1: Define weakness and strength (each in a sentence).

Strength: A strength is a natural ability or quality in which a person excels.

Weakness: A weakness varies from person to person; it is something they find difficult or are not interested in.

Q2: What is your greatest weakness, in your opinion?

I am a little short-tempered and do not have very strong communication skills.

Q3: What do people most often criticize about you? (Any two drawbacks)

- When I know I am right, I do not hold back in front of anyone. People often say I should analyze my surroundings first before taking any action.

- Some say I need to improve my communication skills to express my thoughts better.

Q4: What is the biggest criticism you have ever received?

Teacher: I remain very silent and reserved.

Relatives & Parents: I do not wake up early in the morning.

Siblings: I eat too much fast food and do not exercise regularly.

Friends: Sometimes, I can be a little careless.

Q5: What do you find are the most difficult decisions to make, and why?

I find it difficult to decide whether to talk to strangers or not. I often feel confused in such situations.

Q6: How do you handle failure?

Whenever I face failure, I talk to my mother about everything that went wrong. She motivates and encourages me to do my best next time.

Q7: How do you handle success?

I share my success with my family. Seeing my parents happy is all that matters to me.

Q8: How do you view yourself among the following options?

I see myself as both social and reserved.

Q9: Who do you compare yourself to, and why?

I compare myself to my parents because they are good at managing social situations. I, on the other hand, often feel reserved around relatives. However, when I am comfortable, I perform at my best.

Q10: What major challenges have you faced so far, and how did you handle them?

As an introvert, one of my biggest challenges was when my class teacher in 11th grade made me the class monitor. However, I took the responsibility seriously and completed all my tasks.

Q11: What was the biggest compliment you have received so far?

My teachers have often said that I am a very disciplined student.

Q12: What have you learned from your mistakes?

I have learned that one should not trust or expect too much from others.

Q13: What motivates you?

The desire to improve myself and achieve my goals motivates me to work hard every day.

Q14: What is your greatest strength?

My greatest strengths are discipline and hard work.

Q15: How will your strengths help you progress in life?

Because of my discipline and hard work, I was once the School Discipline Captain and am now leading two PBL projects.

Q16: What have you achieved so far based on your strengths?

Through my discipline and hard work, I have learned valuable skills like Full Stack Development, C, and C++.
